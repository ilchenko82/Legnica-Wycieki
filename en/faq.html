To ensure smooth and quick diagnostics, please take care of a few details before our arrival:

1. **Access to installations**: Please clear the area and provide easy access to the main water shut-off valves, heating manifolds, water meter, and boiler.

2. **Turn off heating**: For suspected underfloor or central heating leaks, we recommend turning off the heating 1-2 hours before our visit. This cools the floor, which is essential to get a clear thermal image once the system is restarted.

3. **Availability**: Please ensure a decision-maker is present who knows the installation's history and can point out where the first signs of moisture appeared.

There are several warning signs to look out for:

1. **Pressure drop in the boiler**: If you have to regularly refill the heating system with water, there is likely a leak.

2. **Water meter movement**: Close all taps, turn off the washing machine and dishwasher, and then check the meter dial. If the indicator is still moving or digits increase, water is escaping uncontrollably.

3. **Moisture and musty smell**: Spots on walls or ceilings, peeling wallpaper, bubbling paint, and a distinct damp smell are clear warning signs.

4. **Noise in pipes**: A quiet, steady hissing sound of water heard at night when no one is using the installation often means a cracked pipe.

Quick and thoughtful action minimizes losses and facilitates the compensation procedure:

1. **Close the main valve**: Shut off the water supply to the building or apartment (usually near the water meter).

2. **Turn off electrical devices**: If water is flooding sockets or wires, safely disconnect the power at the fuse box.

3. **Document the damage**: Take photos and record short videos showing fresh spots, stains, and damaged property. This will be crucial evidence for the insurer.
